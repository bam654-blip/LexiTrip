Tide pooling tip: Check low tide chart for Haystack Rock — purple sea urchins, starfish, anemones. Go at -0.5 ft or lower. One of the best kid activities on the whole coast.

Afternoon🏖 Beach all day
Evening🎆 Fireworks over Haystack Rock — 10pm
Tip: Claim your beach spot early. Bring layers — Oregon coast nights are genuinely cold even in July. Haystack Rock lit up with fireworks behind it is one of the most beautiful things you'll see all trip.
Hike
Sunday, July 5
Ecola State Park
▼
LocationEcola State Park, Cannon Beach, OR 97110
HikeClatsop Loop or Indian Beach Trail — exposed clifftop views over sea stacks. Indian Beach has a sheltered cove great for kids after the hike.
Drive~15 min from Ashore Hotel · $5/vehicle day-use fee
Tip: Go early before fog burns off — misty clifftops over the Pacific are otherworldly. Keep a close eye on the youngest near cliff edges.

Sea glass tip: Manzanita northern end near the creek outlet — best pickings at low tide.

Hike
Wednesday, July 8
Devils Punch Bowl + Seal Rock
▼
Stop 1Devils Punch Bowl — collapsed sea cave that churns like a cauldron at high tide. Dramatic. Go at high tide for full effect.
Stop 2Seal Rock State Recreation Site — great tide pools, sometimes actual seals on rocks. Good sea glass too.
Drive~30 min south of Looking Glass Inn on US-101
Tide tip: Devils Punch Bowl = high tide. Seal Rock tide pools = low tide. Check tideschart.com and plan your order accordingly.
Sea Glass
Thursday, July 9
Otter Rock + Moolack Beach
▼
Stop 1Otter Rock — small cove, excellent glass hunting and beautiful views
Stop 2Moolack Beach — one of the best sea glass beaches on the Oregon coast. Walk the northern end near the old landfill area. Go early.
BonusAsk hotel about Lincoln City's "Finders Keepers" program — city hides hand-blown glass floats on the beach year-round!
Pro tip: Lowest tides = most glass. Check tideschart.com for Newport OR. Overcast mornings reduce glare — actually ideal for spotting glass.

Wildlife
Sunday, July 12
Sea Lion Cave
▼
Location91560 US-101, Florence, OR 97439 · ~20 min north of Yachats
📍 Open in Maps
WhatAmerica's largest sea cave — 12 stories tall, football field wide. Elevator 300 ft down a cliff. In July sea lions are on outer cliffs, but you'll see and hear them. Absolutely not a tourist trap.
Note⚠️ 37 steps + sloped trail before elevator — no strollers
Bring a light jacket — the cave is cold year-round

Travel Day
Sunday, July 13
Yachats → Crescent City, CA
▼
Drive~4 hrs south on US-101 — scenic coastal highway the whole way
En RouteSamuel H. Boardman Scenic Corridor — between Brookings and Gold Beach, OR. Stop at Natural Bridges viewpoint — stunning sea arches. Multiple pullouts along 12 miles. Allow 1.5 hrs just for stops here.

Redwoods
Monday, July 14
Grove of Titans + Stout Grove
▼
Stop 1Grove of Titans — Mill Creek Trailhead, Howland Hill Rd, Crescent City, CA
1.8-mile easy trail. Raised iron walkway through ancient giants including Lost Monarch — 320 ft tall. Go early morning before crowds.
📍 Open in Maps
Stop 2Stout Grove — Jedediah Smith Redwoods, ~10 min away. Short easy loop through pristine old-growth. Do both in one morning.
Tip: Drive Howland Hill Rd — narrow gravel but spectacular. From Oregon, Stout Grove comes first — do it, then Grove of Titans. Both take ~3 hrs with kids.

▼
LocationPrairie Creek Redwoods State Park, ~50 min south of Crescent City
What50-ft walls of ferns, stream crossings, Jurassic Park vibes. ~1-mile loop. Kids absolutely love it.

🌋 Crater Lake
Saturday, July 19
Rim Drive + South Rim Trails
▼
Main EventCrater Lake Rim Drive — 33-mile loop. The lake is 1,943 ft deep and a blue that doesn't look real. Arrive at the rim early — entrance lines form by 11am in July.
Must StopSinnott Memorial Overlook at Rim Village — touch-the-relief-map, geological history, eastern lake view. Pick up Junior Ranger booklets here!
Hikes🌸 Castle Crest Wildflower Loop (0.4 mi)🌊 Sun Notch (0.5 mi, Phantom Ship views)💧 Plaikni Falls (2 mi, easy)
Fee$35/vehicle · Or America the Beautiful Annual Pass $80
Note⚠️ Cleetwood Cove Trail + boat tours closed through ~2028
Tip: Bring real layers — genuinely cold at the rim even in late July. Keep youngest back from cliff edges — barriers are low. The blue of this lake will stop everyone mid-sentence.
🌋 Crater Lake
Sunday, July 20
North Rim + Watchman Peak
▼
TodayDeeper exploration day. North Rim typically opens mid-July — drive it for a completely different perspective.
Best HikeWatchman Peak Trail — 1.4 mi, 420 ft gain. Summit lookout tower, 360° views of entire caldera and Cascades. The exposed hike of Crater Lake. Doable for kids 7+.
Also🌋 Pumice Desert roadside stop🌲 Annie Creek Canyon (1.7 mi loop, shaded gorge)
EveningDinner at Crater Lake Lodge dining room if staying there — book reservations. Or Mazama Village for casual.
→ Bend
Monday, July 21

Drive~1.5 hrs north on OR-97. One of the easiest drives of the whole trip. Arrive in Bend by noon.

🧗 Smith Rock
Tuesday, July 22
Smith Rock State Park
▼
Location9241 NE Crooked River Dr, Terrebonne, OR 97760 · ~30 min north of Bend
📍 Open in Maps
Misery RidgeMisery Ridge Trail — 4.4-mile loop, 925 ft gain, completely exposed, no shade. Steep staircases, big switchbacks, jaw-dropping views. Best exposed hike on this entire trip. Start by 7am — hits 100°F+ by midday.
For KidsRiver Trail — flat 2.5-mile path along the Crooked River. Shaded in spots, great for watching climbers on the walls above.
ClimbingOver 1,800 routes. Bring your own gear or rent in Bend. Monkey Face (350-ft tuff spire) is right on the Misery Ridge loop — kids can watch from the trail below.
Fee$10–12/vehicle · oregonstateparks.org
Watch Out⚠️ Rattlesnakes common near river and rocks in summer
⚠️ Zero shade on Misery Ridge — start early, bring lots of water
Climber tip: You'll walk past the base of Monkey Face on the loop — seeing climbers on the wall with the kids watching is a genuinely memorable moment. Bend has a world-class climbing community. You'll want to come back without the kids someday.
Bend Day
Wednesday, July 23
Deschutes Float + Town
▼
RiverFloat the Deschutes River — rent inner tubes at Tumalo Creek Kayak & Canoe or Sun Country Tours. The Bend Whitewater Park float is a local classic for all ages.
HikeTumalo Falls — 97-ft waterfall, 5 min drive from downtown. Easy 10-min walk to base. Beautiful and easy for all ages.
TownOld Mill District — restaurants, ice cream, shops right on the river. Drake Park along Mirror Pond for a lovely evening walk.
ClimbingBend Rock Gym — 15 auto-belays, air-conditioned. Great afternoon option if it's too hot outside.

En RouteOptional: Timberline Lodge on Mt. Hood — 30-min detour off US-26. Kids touch actual snow on a volcano in July. Only worth it with a late afternoon flight.
